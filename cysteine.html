Types of Amino Acids

Cysteine (Cys, C)

Type:
Nonpolar (Hydrophobic)
Function:
Cysteine is important for protein structure due to its ability to form disulfide bonds, which stabilize protein structures. It also plays a role in detoxification and acts as a precursor to the antioxidant glutathione, protecting cells from oxidative stress.
Sources:
Cysteine is found in high-protein foods like chicken, turkey, eggs, and dairy products. Plant-based sources include legumes, nuts, and seeds.
Additional Info:
Cysteine is a semi-essential amino acid that is particularly important for the production of keratin in the skin and hair. It also helps in the absorption of zinc and selenium, which are crucial for various bodily functions.
