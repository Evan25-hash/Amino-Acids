Types of Amino Acids

Isoleucine (Ile, I)

Type:
Nonpolar (Hydrophobic)
Function:
Isoleucine is essential for protein synthesis and plays a role in muscle metabolism. It helps to regulate blood sugar levels, energy production, and is involved in the immune response.
Sources:
Isoleucine is found in high amounts in animal products such as meat, fish, eggs, and dairy. Plant sources include legumes, nuts, and seeds.
Additional Info:
Isoleucine is one of the three branched-chain amino acids (BCAAs) and is crucial for the repair of muscle tissues. It also supports energy production during intense physical activity.
