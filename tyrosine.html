Types of Amino Acids

Tyrosine (Tyr, Y)

Type:
Polar (Hydrophilic)
Function:
Tyrosine is crucial for the production of several important substances in the body, including neurotransmitters such as dopamine, norepinephrine, and epinephrine. It is also involved in the synthesis of thyroid hormones and melanin, the pigment responsible for skin and hair color.
Sources:
Tyrosine can be obtained from a variety of foods, including meat, dairy products, nuts, seeds, and soy products. It can also be synthesized from another amino acid called phenylalanine.
Additional Info:
Tyrosine is considered a non-essential amino acid because it can be synthesized from phenylalanine. However, under certain conditions such as stress or illness, the body might require additional tyrosine from the diet.
