Types of Amino Acids

Glutamine (Gln, Q)

Type:
Polar (Hydrophilic)
Function:
Glutamine is vital for the synthesis of proteins and plays a key role in cellular metabolism. It helps with nitrogen transport and contributes to maintaining a balanced pH in the body. Glutamine is also important for immune function and gut health.
Sources:
Glutamine is found in various foods, including meat, fish, dairy products, and some plant-based sources such as beans, nuts, and cabbage.
Additional Info:
As a non-essential amino acid, glutamine can be synthesized by the body, but it is especially important during periods of stress or illness. It supports the health of the intestinal lining and can help with recovery after intense physical activity.
