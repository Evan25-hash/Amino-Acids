Types of Amino Acids

Methionine (Met, M)

Type:
Nonpolar (Hydrophobic)
Function:
Methionine is essential for protein synthesis and acts as a precursor for other amino acids. It plays a role in the metabolism of fats and the formation of important molecules in the body.
Sources:
Methionine is found in high amounts in meat, fish, eggs, and dairy products. Plant sources include nuts, seeds, and legumes.
Additional Info:
Methionine contains a sulfur atom and is crucial for the synthesis of proteins and other important molecules. It also helps with detoxification processes in the body.
