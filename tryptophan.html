Types of Amino Acids

Tryptophan (Trp, W)

Type:
Nonpolar (Hydrophobic)
Function:
Tryptophan is essential for protein synthesis and acts as a precursor for serotonin, a neurotransmitter that affects mood, sleep, and appetite. It is also involved in the production of niacin (vitamin B3).
Sources:
Tryptophan is found in high amounts in protein-rich foods such as turkey, chicken, milk, cheese, nuts, and seeds. It is also present in some plant-based proteins.
Additional Info:
Tryptophan is important for regulating mood and sleep. It is often used in supplements to help with sleep disorders and mood improvements. It is a precursor to melatonin, a hormone that regulates sleep cycles.
