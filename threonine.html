Types of Amino Acids

Threonine (Thr, T)

Type:
Polar (Hydrophilic)
Function:
Threonine is important for protein synthesis and is involved in the formation of collagen and elastin, which are essential for the structure and function of connective tissues. It also plays a role in the immune system by supporting proper immune responses and aiding in the production of antibodies.
Sources:
Threonine is found in a variety of foods, including meat, dairy products, eggs, and plant-based sources such as beans, lentils, and nuts.
Additional Info:
Threonine is an essential amino acid, meaning it must be obtained from the diet. It is vital for the health of the digestive system and can help maintain proper protein balance in the body.
