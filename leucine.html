Types of Amino Acids

Leucine (Leu, L)

Type:
Nonpolar (Hydrophobic)
Function:
Leucine is essential for protein synthesis and muscle repair. It helps to regulate blood sugar levels, and is involved in energy production and wound healing.
Sources:
Leucine is abundant in animal products such as meat, fish, eggs, and dairy. Plant sources include legumes, nuts, and seeds.
Additional Info:
Leucine is one of the three branched-chain amino acids (BCAAs) and plays a key role in muscle growth and repair. It also supports energy production during exercise.
