Types of Amino Acids

Glutamic Acid (Glu, E)

Type:
Acidic (Negatively Charged)
Function:
Glutamic acid plays a crucial role in cellular metabolism and is involved in the synthesis of other amino acids. It also functions as a neurotransmitter in the brain, contributing to cognitive functions such as learning and memory.
Sources:
Glutamic acid is found in a variety of protein-rich foods, including meats, poultry, fish, dairy products, and plant-based sources such as beans and legumes.
Additional Info:
Glutamic acid is one of the 20 standard amino acids and is essential for various metabolic processes. It also helps in the regulation of acid-base balance in the body.
