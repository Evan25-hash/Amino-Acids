Types of Amino Acids

Alanine (Ala, A)

Type:
Nonpolar (Hydrophobic)
Function:
Alanine plays a critical role in glucose metabolism by being converted into glucose in the liver, which is vital for energy production. It also supports protein synthesis, contributing to muscle repair and growth, and helps maintain balanced nitrogen levels in the body.
Sources:
Alanine is commonly found in animal products such as meat, poultry, fish, eggs, and dairy products. Plant sources include beans, lentils, nuts, and whole grains.
Additional Info:
As one of the 20 standard amino acids, Alanine is involved in various metabolic processes. It assists in the synthesis of nucleic acids and other vital compounds. Alanine is also used in the production of antibodies and other immune system components.
