Types of Amino Acids

Asparagine (Asn, N)

Type:
Polar (Hydrophilic)
Function:
Asparagine is involved in the synthesis of glycoproteins and the metabolism of ammonia. It plays a crucial role in the biosynthesis of other amino acids and is essential for the proper functioning of the nervous system.
Sources:
Asparagine is found in a variety of foods, including dairy products, beef, poultry, and plant-based sources such as legumes, nuts, and whole grains.
Additional Info:
Asparagine is one of the 20 standard amino acids and is important for the synthesis of neurotransmitters. It also contributes to the production of proteins and plays a role in the cellular response to stress.
