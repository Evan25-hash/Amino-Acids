Types of Amino Acids

Glycine (Gly, G)

Type:
Nonpolar (Hydrophobic)
Function:
Glycine is the smallest amino acid and plays a crucial role in the synthesis of proteins, neurotransmitters, and nucleic acids. It is involved in the formation of collagen, a major structural protein in connective tissues. Glycine also acts as an inhibitory neurotransmitter in the central nervous system, helping to regulate nerve impulses.
Sources:
Glycine is found in high-protein foods like meat, fish, dairy products, and legumes. It is also present in gelatin and bone broth.
Additional Info:
Glycine's small size allows it to fit into tight spaces within protein structures, contributing to the flexibility of proteins. It also has potential therapeutic benefits, such as improving sleep quality and reducing inflammation.
