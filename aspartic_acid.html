Types of Amino Acids

Aspartic Acid (Asp, D)

Type:
Acidic (Negatively Charged)
Function:
Aspartic acid plays a role in the urea cycle and is involved in the synthesis of other amino acids. It also contributes to the production of ATP, which is essential for energy transfer in cells.
Sources:
Aspartic acid is found in various foods, including meats, nuts, seeds, and legumes. It is also present in many protein-rich foods.
Additional Info:
Aspartic acid is one of the 20 standard amino acids and is important for maintaining the proper functioning of the nervous system and supporting overall metabolic health.
