Types of Amino Acids

Lysine (Lys, K)

Type:
Basic (Positively Charged)
Function:
Lysine is essential for protein synthesis and the production of enzymes and hormones. It plays a crucial role in the synthesis of collagen, which is important for healthy skin, bones, and connective tissues.
Sources:
Lysine is found in high-protein foods such as meat, poultry, fish, dairy products, and eggs. Plant-based sources include legumes, nuts, and seeds.
Additional Info:
Lysine is an essential amino acid, meaning it must be obtained through the diet. It is particularly important for proper growth and tissue repair. Lysine supplementation may be used to help with conditions such as herpes simplex virus outbreaks.
