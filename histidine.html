Types of Amino Acids

Histidine (His, H)

Type:
Basic (Positively Charged)
Function:
Histidine is essential for growth and repair of tissues. It plays a role in the production of histamine, a neurotransmitter involved in immune responses, gastric acid secretion, and the regulation of physiological functions in the stomach.
Sources:
Histidine is found in high-protein foods such as meat, poultry, fish, dairy products, and eggs. It can also be obtained from plant-based sources like legumes, nuts, and seeds.
Additional Info:
Histidine is an essential amino acid, meaning it must be obtained through the diet. It is particularly important in infants and growing children due to its role in growth and tissue repair.
