Types of Amino Acids

Phenylalanine (Phe, F)

Type:
Nonpolar (Hydrophobic)
Function:
Phenylalanine is essential for protein synthesis and acts as a precursor for neurotransmitters like tyrosine, dopamine, and norepinephrine. It is important for brain function and mood regulation.
Sources:
Phenylalanine is found in high amounts in protein-rich foods such as meat, fish, eggs, dairy products, and certain plant-based sources like soybeans and nuts.
Additional Info:
Phenylalanine is a precursor to tyrosine, which is involved in the production of neurotransmitters that regulate mood, cognitive function, and stress responses. It is also used in the production of the artificial sweetener aspartame.
